Product Requirements Document (PRD)

Doctor.mx – AI Healthcare Platform for Mexico

Version: 2.0 Status: Advanced Draft Last Updated: May 12, 2025 Classification: Confidential

1. Executive Summary

Doctor.mx is evolving to become the most trusted digital gateway to comprehensive healthcare in Mexico. Version 2.0 expands the platform's reach from a medically oriented chatbot to a connected ecosystem that combines world-class clinical AI, teleconsultation, e-prescription, chronic disease management, preventative wellness, and emergency services focused on measurable clinical and financial outcomes.

Key Objectives

Accessibility: Serve 1 million Mexicans per month by the end of 2026.

Clinical Quality: Achieve ≥93% diagnostic concordance for prevalent pathologies.

Integration: Connect with ≥75% of relevant private providers and 3 public institutions by 2027.

Sustainability: Achieve positive EBITDA in the first quarter of 2027.

2. 2030 Vision

To be the Latin American benchmark for digital health, where every Mexican receives personalized, predictive, and preventive guidance and care, guided by multimodal AI and supported by a network of interconnected professionals, laboratories, pharmacies, and insurers.

3. Strategic Objectives

Timely diagnosis through multimodal AI trained with clinical data from national and underrepresented populations.

Continuity of care through FHIR integration with EHRs, laboratories, pharmacies, and wearable devices.

Chronic disease management focused on diabetes, hypertension, mental health, and maternal and child health.

Trust and security with COFEPRIS, ISO 27001, and INAI certifications by design.

Mixed business model (B2C + B2B + B2G) that combines subscriptions, commissions, and technology licensing.

4. User Segments and Personas

4.1 Consumers (B2C)

Digital urban core: 23-45 years old, smartphone, digital payments.

Rural families: 40-65 years old, intermittent connectivity, freemium offline plan.

Chronic patients: ≥35 years old, high frequency of use, focused on follow-up and adherence.

4.2 Providers and Payers (B2B)

Regional clinics and hospitals (Star Médica, Christus Muguerza).

Pharmacies with doctor's offices (Simi, del Ahorro, Guadalajara).

Insurance companies (GNP, AXA) and corporate self-financing companies.

4.3 Government and NGOs (B2G)

Municipal preventive health programs.

IMSS-Bienestar and INSABI for screening campaigns.

5. Value Proposition

Immediate 24/7 medical guidance with transparent evidence and explanations.

Direct connection with verified professionals and nearby diagnostic services.

Holistic care that integrates prevention, treatment, and follow-up.

Predictable costs with subscription plans and partial insurance coverage.

Robust privacy that adheres to Mexican and international standards.

6. User Experience

6.1 Main Flows

Express AI Consultation: ≤ 3 minutes from the first symptom to the recommendation.

Scheduled teleconsultation with medical history and previous images to expedite triage.

Chronic management (connectivity with glucometers, blood pressure monitors, and wearables).

Smart emergency services that detect warning signs and activate 911 or the Red Cross Network.

6.2 UI/UX Requirements

Multichannel conversation: text, voice, WhatsApp, and USSD (data-free mode).

Context-sensitive design: low-bandwidth mode, high contrast, and easy-to-read.

Languages: Spanish (MX), Border English, and 5 prioritized indigenous languages.

Reliability indicators: confidence bar, scientific source, and last clinical review date.

7. Technical Requirements

7.1 Clinical AI

Foundational models: Spanish LLM + vision + fine-tuned tabular with 2M anonymized Mexican records.

Probabilistic reasoning: Bayes + expert-reinforced decision tree.

Visual and textual explainability engine (B1 reading level).

7.2 Architecture

Microservices on Kubernetes (GCP US-Central region + MX-QRO failover).

GraphQL orchestration API.

FHIR Gateway layer for interoperability.

Segregated databases (Clinical, Analytics, Logs) with AES-256 at-rest encryption.

7.3 Security and Compliance

Granular consent management (OpenID Connect + UMA 2.0).

Immutable auditing (Lightweight Blockchain Hyperledger Fabric for access events).

Semi-annual pen-testing and bug bounty.

8. Feature Map

Phase 1 (0-6 months)

Multimodal AI consultation.

Basic provider and pharmacy network (top 5 chains).

E-prescription and in-app payment.

Clinical performance metrics dashboard.

Phase 2 (7-12 months)

Chronic disease modules.

Integrated labs and imaging studies.

Advanced explainability with 3D simulations.

Corporate plan for SMEs and municipalities.

Phase 3 (13-24 months)

Predictive Preventive AI: population risk model and personalized campaigns.

Health Market-Place: on-demand insurance, devices, and programs.
