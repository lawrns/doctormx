## 🎯 **Objective**
Define specific types of video consultations suitable for the Mexican healthcare context, mapping use cases, eligibility criteria, and implementation requirements based on patient and doctor personas.

## 🏥 **Video Consultation Types**

### **Type 1: Follow-up Consultations (Consultas de Seguimiento)**

**Definition:**
Scheduled appointments to monitor ongoing treatment, review test results, or assess patient progress for established conditions.

**Use Cases:**
- **Chronic Disease Management**: Diabetes, hypertension, thyroid disorders
- **Post-Treatment Monitoring**: Recovery from surgery, medication adjustments
- **Test Result Reviews**: Lab results, imaging studies discussion
- **Prescription Renewals**: Routine medication refills with assessment

**Patient Personas Best Suited:**
- **María González** (Urban Professional): Convenient follow-ups for busy schedule
- **Roberto Hernández** (Middle-aged Traditional): Chronic condition monitoring
- **Carmen López** (Senior): Diabetes/hypertension management without travel

**Doctor Personas Most Likely to Adopt:**
- **Dr. Patricia Mendoza** (Progressive GP): Efficiency for routine follow-ups
- **Dr. Ana Lucía Vásquez** (Digital Dermatologist): Skin condition monitoring

**Eligibility Criteria:**
- ✅ **Established patient-doctor relationship** (minimum 1 in-person visit)
- ✅ **Stable condition** requiring monitoring, not acute intervention
- ✅ **Clear treatment plan** already established
- ✅ **Patient has reliable internet** and video-capable device
- ❌ **No new symptoms** requiring physical examination
- ❌ **No medication changes** requiring immediate monitoring

**Technical Requirements:**
- **Video Quality**: Standard HD (720p) sufficient
- **Duration**: 10-20 minutes average
- **Documentation**: Access to previous visit notes and test results
- **Integration**: EMR system for medical history review

**Pricing Model:**
- **Private**: $400-800 MXN (30-40% discount from in-person)
- **Insurance**: Negotiated rates with major insurers
- **Public System**: Government-subsidized rates

---

### **Type 2: Initial Consultations (Consultas Iniciales)**

**Definition:**
First-time consultations for non-emergency conditions that can be assessed through visual examination and patient history.

**Use Cases:**
- **Dermatological Conditions**: Rashes, acne, moles, skin lesions
- **Mental Health**: Depression, anxiety, stress management
- **Minor Symptoms**: Cold, flu, allergies, headaches
- **Contraception Counseling**: Birth control options, family planning

**Patient Personas Best Suited:**
- **Ana Sofía Ramírez** (Student): Mental health, contraception, minor illnesses
- **Daniela Morales** (Working Mother): Quick assessment for minor symptoms
- **María González** (Urban Professional): Dermatology, preventive care

**Doctor Personas Most Likely to Adopt:**
- **Dr. Ana Lucía Vásquez** (Digital Dermatologist): Visual diagnosis specialty
- **Dr. Patricia Mendoza** (Progressive GP): Efficient triage for minor conditions

**Eligibility Criteria:**
- ✅ **Non-emergency conditions** (no immediate danger signs)
- ✅ **Visual assessment possible** (skin conditions, general appearance)
- ✅ **Patient can communicate symptoms** clearly
- ✅ **Stable vital signs** (if measurable at home)
- ❌ **No chest pain, difficulty breathing** or other emergency symptoms
- ❌ **No conditions requiring** physical manipulation or auscultation

**Technical Requirements:**
- **Video Quality**: High HD (1080p) for detailed visual assessment
- **Duration**: 15-30 minutes for thorough history taking
- **Documentation**: Comprehensive intake forms, symptom questionnaires
- **Integration**: Prescription system for treatment recommendations

**Pricing Model:**
- **Private**: $600-1,200 MXN (similar to in-person for complexity)
- **Insurance**: Standard consultation rates
- **Student Discounts**: 20-30% reduction for verified students

---

### **Type 3: Specialist Consultations (Consultas de Especialidad)**

**Definition:**
Expert consultations for specific medical conditions requiring specialized knowledge but not physical examination.

**Use Cases:**
- **Endocrinology**: Diabetes management, thyroid disorders
- **Cardiology**: Medication adjustments, lifestyle counseling
- **Psychiatry**: Mental health treatment, medication management
- **Nutrition**: Dietary counseling, weight management

**Patient Personas Best Suited:**
- **Roberto Hernández** (Traditional): Access to specialists without travel
- **Carmen López** (Senior): Specialist care for chronic conditions
- **Luis Miguel Torres** (Rural): Access to city specialists

**Doctor Personas Most Likely to Adopt:**
- **Dr. Patricia Mendoza** (GP): Referring to specialists efficiently
- **Dr. Carlos Rodríguez** (Cardiologist): Follow-up care for stable patients

**Eligibility Criteria:**
- ✅ **Referral from primary care** physician (for new patients)
- ✅ **Stable specialist condition** requiring expert management
- ✅ **Recent diagnostic tests** available for review
- ✅ **Clear treatment goals** established
- ❌ **No acute exacerbations** requiring immediate intervention
- ❌ **No procedures needed** during consultation

**Technical Requirements:**
- **Video Quality**: High HD for detailed assessment
- **Duration**: 30-45 minutes for comprehensive evaluation
- **Documentation**: Specialist templates, referral integration
- **Integration**: Lab results, imaging studies access

**Pricing Model:**
- **Private**: $1,000-2,000 MXN (specialist rates)
- **Insurance**: Specialist copay rates
- **Rural Subsidies**: Government support for underserved areas

---

### **Type 4: Emergency Triage (Triaje de Urgencias)**

**Definition:**
Rapid assessment to determine if immediate emergency care is needed or if condition can be managed remotely.

**Use Cases:**
- **Symptom Assessment**: Fever, pain, minor injuries evaluation
- **Medication Reactions**: Allergy assessment, side effect evaluation
- **Urgent Questions**: Post-procedure concerns, medication clarifications
- **Rural Emergency Support**: Initial assessment before transport

**Patient Personas Best Suited:**
- **Luis Miguel Torres** (Rural): Remote areas with limited emergency access
- **Daniela Morales** (Working Mother): Quick triage for children's symptoms
- **All personas**: After-hours urgent questions

**Doctor Personas Most Likely to Adopt:**
- **Dr. Miguel Ángel Herrera** (Rural): Support for emergency decisions
- **Dr. Patricia Mendoza** (GP): After-hours patient support

**Eligibility Criteria:**
- ✅ **Non-life-threatening symptoms** requiring professional assessment
- ✅ **Patient conscious and communicating** effectively
- ✅ **Symptoms started recently** (within 24-48 hours)
- ✅ **Access to emergency services** if escalation needed
- ❌ **No loss of consciousness** or severe distress
- ❌ **No chest pain, severe breathing difficulty** or other red flags

**Technical Requirements:**
- **Video Quality**: Standard HD, mobile-optimized
- **Duration**: 5-15 minutes for rapid assessment
- **Documentation**: Triage protocols, escalation procedures
- **Integration**: Emergency services coordination

**Pricing Model:**
- **Private**: $300-600 MXN (urgent care rates)
- **Insurance**: Emergency consultation coverage
- **Rural**: Government-subsidized emergency support

---

### **Type 5: Preventive Care (Atención Preventiva)**

**Definition:**
Health maintenance consultations focused on prevention, health education, and early detection of conditions.

**Use Cases:**
- **Health Screenings**: Risk assessment questionnaires, lifestyle evaluation
- **Vaccination Counseling**: Immunization schedules, travel vaccines
- **Lifestyle Counseling**: Diet, exercise, smoking cessation
- **Health Education**: Disease prevention, wellness programs

**Patient Personas Best Suited:**
- **María González** (Urban Professional): Preventive care for busy lifestyle
- **Ana Sofía Ramírez** (Student): Health education, lifestyle counseling
- **Daniela Morales** (Working Mother): Family health planning

**Doctor Personas Most Likely to Adopt:**
- **Dr. Patricia Mendoza** (Progressive GP): Preventive medicine focus
- **Dr. Ana Lucía Vásquez** (Digital Native): Wellness program integration

**Eligibility Criteria:**
- ✅ **Healthy individuals** seeking preventive care
- ✅ **Established health baseline** from previous visits
- ✅ **Motivated for lifestyle changes** and health improvement
- ✅ **Access to health tracking tools** (apps, wearables)
- ❌ **No active health concerns** requiring immediate attention
- ❌ **No overdue screenings** requiring in-person procedures

**Technical Requirements:**
- **Video Quality**: Standard HD sufficient
- **Duration**: 20-30 minutes for comprehensive counseling
- **Documentation**: Health risk assessments, goal tracking
- **Integration**: Wellness apps, health tracking platforms

**Pricing Model:**
- **Private**: $500-800 MXN (preventive care rates)
- **Insurance**: Preventive care coverage (often 100%)
- **Wellness Programs**: Corporate health plan integration

## 🎯 **Implementation Roadmap**

### **Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)**
- **Focus**: Follow-up consultations
- **Target**: Established patients with chronic conditions
- **Doctors**: Early adopters (Dr. Patricia, Dr. Ana Lucía)
- **Patients**: Urban professionals, tech-savvy users

### **Phase 2: Expansion (Months 4-6)**
- **Focus**: Initial consultations for suitable conditions
- **Target**: New patients with minor, visual conditions
- **Doctors**: Early majority adoption
- **Patients**: Broader demographic inclusion

### **Phase 3: Specialization (Months 7-12)**
- **Focus**: Specialist consultations
- **Target**: Referred patients, chronic disease management
- **Doctors**: Specialist recruitment and training
- **Patients**: Complex cases requiring expert care

### **Phase 4: Comprehensive (Year 2+)**
- **Focus**: Emergency triage and preventive care
- **Target**: Complete service offering
- **Doctors**: Full platform adoption
- **Patients**: All suitable use cases covered
