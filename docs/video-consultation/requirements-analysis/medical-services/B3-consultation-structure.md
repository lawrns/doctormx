## 🎯 **Objective**
Design standardized video consultation flow, duration guidelines, and quality protocols for different consultation types and medical specialties in the Mexican healthcare context.

## 🏗️ **Standard Consultation Structure**

### **📋 Pre-Consultation Phase (5-10 minutes)**

#### **Patient Preparation Checklist**
- **Technical Setup**: Camera, microphone, lighting verification
- **Environment**: Quiet, private space with good lighting
- **Documentation**: Insurance card, ID, previous medical records ready
- **Symptom Preparation**: Written list of symptoms, questions, current medications

#### **Doctor Preparation Checklist**
- **Patient Review**: Medical history, previous visits, lab results review
- **Technical Setup**: Platform login, camera/audio test, EMR access
- **Documentation**: Consultation template, prescription pad ready
- **Emergency Protocols**: Local emergency contacts, escalation procedures

#### **Platform Pre-Flight Check**
- **Connection Quality**: Internet speed test (minimum 2 Mbps upload/download)
- **Device Compatibility**: Camera resolution check (minimum 720p)
- **Audio Quality**: Microphone and speaker test
- **Security Verification**: End-to-end encryption confirmation

---

### **🎬 Consultation Flow by Type**

#### **Type 1: Follow-up Consultation (10-20 minutes)**

**⏰ Minute 0-2: Opening & Verification**
- **Patient Identification**: Verify name, DOB, medical record number
- **Consent Confirmation**: Video recording consent, privacy acknowledgment
- **Technical Check**: Audio/video quality confirmation
- **Chief Concern**: "How are you feeling since our last visit?"

**⏰ Minute 2-8: Assessment & Review**
- **Symptom Review**: Changes since last visit, medication compliance
- **Vital Signs**: Patient-reported or home-measured vitals
- **Lab Results**: Review recent test results if applicable
- **Visual Assessment**: General appearance, specific areas of concern

**⏰ Minute 8-15: Treatment Plan**
- **Medication Adjustments**: Dosage changes, new prescriptions
- **Lifestyle Recommendations**: Diet, exercise, lifestyle modifications
- **Follow-up Planning**: Next appointment scheduling, monitoring plan
- **Patient Education**: Condition explanation, warning signs

**⏰ Minute 15-20: Documentation & Closure**
- **Prescription Sending**: Electronic prescription to pharmacy
- **Summary Provision**: Visit summary email to patient
- **Questions**: Final patient questions and clarifications
- **Next Steps**: Clear action items and follow-up timeline

---

#### **Type 2: Initial Consultation (15-30 minutes)**

**⏰ Minute 0-3: Opening & History**
- **Patient Identification**: Full verification process
- **Chief Complaint**: "What brings you here today?"
- **History of Present Illness**: Detailed symptom exploration
- **Technical Optimization**: Camera positioning for best visualization

**⏰ Minute 3-12: Comprehensive Assessment**
- **Medical History**: Past medical history, surgeries, allergies
- **Medication Review**: Current medications, supplements, dosages
- **Family History**: Relevant family medical history
- **Social History**: Smoking, alcohol, occupation, lifestyle

**⏰ Minute 12-20: Physical Examination (Virtual)**
- **General Appearance**: Overall health assessment
- **Vital Signs**: Patient-reported temperature, blood pressure if available
- **Focused Examination**: Specialty-specific visual examination
- **Symptom Demonstration**: Patient shows affected areas/symptoms

**⏰ Minute 20-25: Diagnosis & Treatment**
- **Assessment**: Clinical impression and differential diagnosis
- **Treatment Plan**: Medications, lifestyle changes, procedures
- **Patient Education**: Condition explanation, prognosis discussion
- **Safety Netting**: When to seek immediate care, red flag symptoms

**⏰ Minute 25-30: Follow-up & Documentation**
- **Follow-up Scheduling**: Next appointment timing and type
- **Prescription Management**: Electronic prescriptions sent
- **Patient Resources**: Educational materials, support groups
- **Contact Information**: How to reach doctor between visits

---

#### **Type 3: Specialist Consultation (30-45 minutes)**

**⏰ Minute 0-5: Specialist Introduction**
- **Referral Review**: Primary care referral reason and urgency
- **Specialist Expertise**: Doctor's qualifications and experience
- **Consultation Goals**: What patient hopes to achieve
- **Technical Setup**: High-quality video for detailed assessment

**⏰ Minute 5-20: Detailed History**
- **Specialty-Focused History**: Detailed exploration of specialty-related symptoms
- **Previous Treatments**: What has been tried, what worked/didn't work
- **Diagnostic Review**: Previous tests, imaging, lab results
- **Impact Assessment**: How condition affects daily life, work, relationships

**⏰ Minute 20-35: Specialist Assessment**
- **Expert Examination**: Specialty-specific visual assessment techniques
- **Diagnostic Interpretation**: Expert analysis of available data
- **Additional Testing**: Recommendations for further diagnostic work
- **Treatment Options**: Comprehensive treatment plan discussion

**⏰ Minute 35-40: Specialist Recommendations**
- **Primary Recommendation**: Preferred treatment approach with rationale
- **Alternative Options**: Other treatment possibilities with pros/cons
- **Prognosis Discussion**: Expected outcomes, timeline for improvement
- **Coordination**: Communication plan with primary care doctor

**⏰ Minute 40-45: Follow-up Planning**
- **Specialist Follow-up**: When to return to specialist
- **Primary Care Coordination**: What primary care should monitor
- **Patient Resources**: Specialist-specific educational materials
- **Emergency Planning**: When to seek urgent specialist care

---

### **⚡ Emergency Triage Consultation (5-15 minutes)**

**⏰ Minute 0-2: Rapid Assessment**
- **Immediate Danger**: Life-threatening symptoms assessment
- **Vital Signs**: Current temperature, pulse, breathing if measurable
- **Pain Scale**: 1-10 pain assessment if applicable
- **Consciousness Level**: Alert, oriented, responsive assessment

**⏰ Minute 2-8: Symptom Evaluation**
- **Onset**: When symptoms started, progression pattern
- **Severity**: Comparison to previous similar episodes
- **Associated Symptoms**: Related symptoms that may indicate urgency
- **Red Flags**: Emergency warning signs screening

**⏰ Minute 8-12: Triage Decision**
- **Emergency Referral**: Immediate 911/emergency room direction
- **Urgent Care**: Same-day in-person care recommendation
- **Scheduled Care**: Regular appointment within 24-48 hours
- **Home Management**: Safe home care with monitoring instructions

**⏰ Minute 12-15: Safety Planning**
- **Clear Instructions**: Specific next steps for patient
- **Warning Signs**: When to escalate care immediately
- **Follow-up**: Check-in call or message timeline
- **Documentation**: Detailed triage decision rationale

## 📊 **Quality Protocols & Standards**

### **Technical Quality Standards**
- **Video Resolution**: Minimum 720p HD, preferred 1080p for dermatology
- **Audio Quality**: Clear speech without echo or distortion
- **Connection Stability**: Less than 5% packet loss, stable throughout consultation
- **Platform Reliability**: 99.9% uptime, immediate technical support available

### **Medical Quality Standards**
- **Documentation**: Complete consultation notes within 2 hours
- **Prescription Accuracy**: Double-check all medications and dosages
- **Follow-up Compliance**: Clear next steps and timeline communication
- **Emergency Protocols**: Immediate escalation procedures for urgent situations

### **Patient Experience Standards**
- **Wait Time**: Maximum 5 minutes past scheduled appointment time
- **Communication**: Clear, jargon-free explanations
- **Respect**: Cultural sensitivity and patient dignity maintained
- **Privacy**: HIPAA-compliant secure platform usage

### **Doctor Performance Standards**
- **Punctuality**: On-time start for 95% of appointments
- **Completion Rate**: 98% of scheduled consultations completed
- **Patient Satisfaction**: Minimum 4.5/5 rating average
- **Clinical Outcomes**: Appropriate care with minimal adverse events

## 📋 **Documentation Templates**

### **Standard SOAP Note Structure**
**Subjective:**
- Chief complaint and history of present illness
- Review of systems relevant to complaint
- Current medications and allergies

**Objective:**
- Virtual physical examination findings
- Vital signs (patient-reported or measured)
- Review of available diagnostic tests

**Assessment:**
- Clinical impression and differential diagnosis
- Severity assessment and risk stratification

**Plan:**
- Treatment recommendations and prescriptions
- Follow-up instructions and timeline
- Patient education provided

### **Consultation-Specific Additions**
- **Technology Quality**: Video/audio quality assessment
- **Patient Compliance**: Ability to follow virtual examination instructions
- **Safety Netting**: Emergency contact information and escalation plan
- **Coordination**: Communication with other healthcare providers

## 🚨 **Emergency Escalation Protocols**

### **Immediate Emergency (Call 911)**
- **Chest Pain**: Severe, crushing, radiating chest pain
- **Breathing Difficulty**: Severe shortness of breath, unable to speak
- **Neurological**: Stroke symptoms, severe headache, confusion
- **Trauma**: Serious injury, bleeding, loss of consciousness

### **Urgent Care (Same Day)**
- **Moderate Pain**: Severe but stable pain requiring evaluation
- **Infection Signs**: High fever, severe symptoms suggesting serious infection
- **Mental Health Crisis**: Suicidal ideation, severe psychiatric symptoms
- **Medication Reactions**: Serious allergic reactions, adverse drug effects

### **Scheduled Care (24-48 hours)**
- **Worsening Symptoms**: Gradual deterioration of chronic conditions
- **New Symptoms**: New concerning symptoms requiring evaluation
- **Medication Issues**: Side effects, ineffective treatment
- **Follow-up Needs**: Routine monitoring of stable conditions
