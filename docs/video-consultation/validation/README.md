# ✅ **Validation Framework**

## 📋 **Overview**
This framework ensures all Phase 1.1 deliverables meet acceptance criteria and quality standards before proceeding to implementation.

## 🎯 **Validation Process**

### **Three-Stage Validation**
1. **Self-Validation**: Task owner validates against acceptance criteria
2. **Peer Review**: Cross-functional team member reviews deliverable  
3. **Stakeholder Approval**: Final approval from designated stakeholder

## 📋 **Validation Checklists**

### **Category A: Stakeholder Analysis**

#### **A.1 Stakeholder Mapping Validation**
**Self-Validation Checklist**:
- [ ] Minimum 8 stakeholder types identified
- [ ] Power/influence level defined for each
- [ ] Engagement strategy documented
- [ ] Champions and detractors identified
- [ ] Communication plan established

**Peer Review Checklist**:
- [ ] Stakeholder identification is comprehensive
- [ ] Power/influence analysis is realistic
- [ ] Engagement strategies are actionable
- [ ] No critical stakeholders missed

**Stakeholder Approval Criteria**:
- [ ] Product Manager confirms strategic alignment
- [ ] Engagement strategies approved for execution
- [ ] Resource allocation for stakeholder management confirmed

#### **A.2 Patient Research Validation**
**Self-Validation Checklist**:
- [ ] 4-6 distinct patient personas created
- [ ] Demographics representative of Mexican population
- [ ] Technology barriers clearly identified
- [ ] Healthcare behavior patterns documented
- [ ] Journey validated with real users

**Peer Review Checklist**:
- [ ] Personas are distinct and well-defined
- [ ] Research methodology is sound
- [ ] Cultural considerations are addressed
- [ ] Technology barriers are realistic

**Stakeholder Approval Criteria**:
- [ ] UX Lead confirms personas align with target market
- [ ] Research sample size is adequate
- [ ] Insights are actionable for design decisions

#### **A.3 Doctor Research Validation**
**Self-Validation Checklist**:
- [ ] 3-4 distinct doctor personas created
- [ ] Different specialties and practice types covered
- [ ] Technology adoption patterns identified
- [ ] Workflow requirements documented
- [ ] Incentives and resistances mapped

**Peer Review Checklist**:
- [ ] Doctor personas cover key segments
- [ ] Workflow analysis is thorough
- [ ] Technology adoption barriers identified
- [ ] Incentive structure is realistic

**Stakeholder Approval Criteria**:
- [ ] Medical Specialist validates clinical accuracy
- [ ] Workflow requirements are implementable
- [ ] Adoption strategy is viable

## 🔍 **Quality Standards**

### **Documentation Quality**
- **Completeness**: All sections filled with meaningful content
- **Clarity**: Clear, concise, and unambiguous language
- **Accuracy**: Information is factual and up-to-date
- **Actionability**: Insights lead to clear next steps

### **Research Quality**
- **Methodology**: Appropriate research methods used
- **Sample Size**: Adequate representation for conclusions
- **Validation**: Findings validated with real users/data
- **Bias Mitigation**: Researcher bias acknowledged and mitigated

### **Technical Quality**
- **Feasibility**: Technical solutions are implementable
- **Scalability**: Solutions can handle expected load
- **Integration**: Fits with existing architecture
- **Performance**: Meets performance requirements

## 🚨 **Escalation Process**

### **When to Escalate**
- Stakeholder approval is denied
- Critical compliance issues identified
- Technical feasibility concerns arise
- Timeline impact is significant

### **Escalation Path**
1. **Level 1**: Project Manager
2. **Level 2**: Technical Lead + Product Owner
3. **Level 3**: Executive Sponsor

### **Escalation Documentation**
- Issue description and impact
- Attempted resolution steps
- Recommended solution
- Resource requirements
