# Operational Framework for Building a Physician Network in Mexico
## AI-Powered Telemedicine Service

---

## Executive Summary

This document provides a comprehensive operational framework for building and managing a physician network in Mexico for an AI-powered telemedicine service. The service model involves AI conducting initial patient intake and triage, referring patients to appropriate physicians, and doctors providing diagnosis and treatment via WhatsApp/video at $499 MXN per consultation or $1,999 for a 6-month subscription.

**Key Market Context:**
- Mexico has approximately 2.5 physicians per 1,000 inhabitants (bottom quartile among OECD countries)
- ~45% of doctors in Mexico now conduct virtual consultations
- Average GP salary: MXN 300,000-600,000/year (MXN 25,000-50,000/month)
- Telemedicine consultations typically cost MXN 100-500 ($6-$30 USD)
- Regulatory framework is evolving with January 2026 General Health Law reforms

## 1. PHYSICIAN RECRUITMENT STRATEGIES

### 1.1 Licensed Physician Requirements in Mexico

**Primary Credential: Cédula Profesional**
- All physicians must hold an active professional license (cédula profesional) issued by the Dirección General de Profesiones (Secretaría de Educación Pública)
- License verification available through Mexico's National Registry of Professionals online
- COFEPRIS requires doctors to display their cédula number outside their office and in digital ads

**Specialty Certification (CONACEM)**
- Specialists can obtain additional certification through CONACEM (Comité Normativo Nacional de Consejos de Especialidades Médicas)
- Optional but highly recommended for specialists
- Verify certification status on CONACEM website

### 1.2 Types of Doctors Needed

**Primary Care (70-80% of network)**
- General Practitioners (Médicos Generales/Médicos Cirujanos)
- Family Medicine specialists
- Internal Medicine physicians

**Specialists (20-30% of network)**
- Cardiology
- Dermatology
- Endocrinology (diabetes management)
- Gynecology
- Pediatrics
- Psychiatry
- Gastroenterology

**Recommended Initial Ratio:**
- 3-4 GPs per specialist
- Minimum 50 physicians to launch with adequate coverage

### 1.3 Geographic Distribution Requirements

**Priority Markets (High demand, good connectivity):**
1. Mexico City (CDMX) - Largest market
2. Monterrey - High private healthcare spending
3. Guadalajara - Tech-savvy population
4. Puebla - Growing middle class
5. Querétaro - High income per capita

**Secondary Markets:**
- Tijuana, Mérida, Cancún, Toluca, León

**Rural Strategy:**
- Telemedicine is particularly valuable in areas difficult to access
- Partner with local pharmacies for prescription fulfillment
- Consider differential pricing for underserved regions

### 1.4 Recruitment Channels

**Digital Platforms:**
- Doctoralia México (11M+ users, 290K+ healthcare providers)
- LinkedIn healthcare professional groups
- LatHire (Latin America medical practitioner recruitment)
- MediKali (healthcare professional network)

**Professional Networks:**
- Mexican Medical Association (Asociación Médica Mexicana)
- State medical societies
- Medical school alumni networks
- Hospital physician networks

**Direct Outreach:**
- Target doctors working in public sector (IMSS/ISSSTE) seeking supplemental income
- Recent graduates completing social service
- Physicians in private practice seeking additional revenue streams

### 1.5 Employment Models

**Model Comparison:**

| Model | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|-------|------|------|----------|
| **Full-time** | Dedicated availability, quality control | Higher fixed costs | Launch phase, core team |
| **Part-time** | Flexibility, lower risk | Limited availability | Established networks |
| **On-demand** | Maximum flexibility, pay-per-consultation | Response time variability | Scale phase, specialist coverage |
| **Hybrid** | Balance of availability and cost | Complex scheduling | Recommended model |

**Recommended Approach: Hybrid Model**
- Core team: 20-30% full-time physicians
- Extended network: 70-80% part-time/on-demand physicians
- Minimum commitment: 4-hour blocks for part-time doctors

## 2. COMPENSATION MODELS

### 2.1 Market Benchmarks

**Traditional Practice Income (Annual):**
- General Practitioner (public): MXN 120,000-300,000
- General Practitioner (private): MXN 300,000-600,000
- Specialist (public): MXN 300,000-800,000
- Specialist (private): MXN 700,000-2,500,000+

**Telemedicine Physician Salary Data:**
- Average telemedicine physician: MXN 800,837/year (MXN 385/hour)
- Typical telemedicine consultation: MXN 100-500 ($6-$30 USD)

### 2.2 Compensation Structure Options

**Option A: Per-Consultation Model (Recommended for Launch)**
| Physician Type | Per-Consultation Rate | Est. Monthly Income* |
|----------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| General Practitioner | MXN 150-250 | MXN 18,000-30,000 |
| Specialist | MXN 300-450 | MXN 36,000-54,000 |

*Based on 120 consultations/month for GPs, 120 for specialists

**Option B: Hourly Model**
| Physician Type | Hourly Rate | Notes |
|----------------|-------------|-------|
| General Practitioner | MXN 200-350 | For scheduled blocks |
| Specialist | MXN 400-600 | Premium for expertise |

**Option C: Revenue Sharing**
- Platform: 40-50% of consultation fee
- Physician: 50-60% of consultation fee
- Example at $499 MXN consultation:
  - GP receives: MXN 200-250
  - Platform retains: MXN 249-299

**Option D: Subscription-Based (for 6-month plan)**
- Physician receives: MXN 150-200 per active consultation
- Platform manages patient acquisition costs
- Incentivizes quality for retention

### 2.3 Recommended Compensation Framework

**For $499 MXN Single Consultation:**
- GP receives: MXN 200 (40%)
- Specialist receives: MXN 275 (55%)
- Platform: MXN 224-299

**For $1,999 MXN 6-Month Subscription:**
- Assume average 3 consultations per subscriber
- GP receives: MXN 150 per consultation
- Specialist receives: MXN 200 per consultation
- Platform manages patient acquisition and retention

**Additional Incentives:**
- Quality bonus: MXN 500-1,000/month for 4.5+ star ratings
- Availability bonus: MXN 1,000-2,000/month for 40+ hours online
- Patient retention bonus: MXN 100 per returning patient

### 2.4 Payment Terms

- Weekly payments for per-consultation model
- Bi-weekly for hourly model
- Monthly reconciliation with performance bonuses
- Payment via bank transfer (SPEI) or digital wallets (Mercado Pago, Clip)

## 3. LICENSING & CREDENTIALING

### 3.1 License Verification Process

**Required Documentation:**
1. Cédula Profesional (professional license)
2. Medical degree certificate (título profesional)
3. Specialty certification (if applicable) - CONACEM
4. CURP (Unique Population Registry Code)
5. RFC (Tax ID)
6. Proof of address
7. Government-issued ID

**Verification Steps:**
1. Verify cédula through National Registry of Professionals
2. Confirm specialty certification through CONACEM
3. Validate no sanctions through COFEPRIS
4. Background check (certificate of no criminal record)
5. Reference checks (2-3 professional references)

### 3.2 Malpractice Insurance Requirements

**Current Market in Mexico:**
- Malpractice insurance is NOT legally required but STRONGLY recommended
- Many hospitals require malpractice insurance for admitting privileges
- Limited legal recourse for patients makes insurance critical for protection

**Recommended Coverage:**
| Coverage Type | Recommended Limits | Est. Annual Cost |
|--------------|-------------------|------------------|
| General Practice | MXN 2-5 million per claim | MXN 15,000-30,000 |
| Specialists | MXN 5-10 million per claim | MXN 30,000-80,000 |

**Insurance Providers in Mexico:**
- GNP (Grupo Nacional Provincial)
- AXA Seguros
- MetLife México
- Seguros Monterrey New York Life

**Platform Options:**
1. Require physicians to carry individual malpractice insurance
2. Provide group malpractice insurance (recommended for better rates)
3. Hybrid: Platform provides base coverage, physicians can supplement

### 3.3 Continuing Education Requirements

**Mexican Requirements:**
- No federal mandatory CME (Continuing Medical Education) hours
- Some state medical societies have requirements
- CONACEM certification requires periodic recertification

**Platform Requirements (Recommended):**
- 20 hours/year telemedicine-specific training
- Annual platform recertification
- Quarterly updates on clinical protocols
- AI collaboration training (unique to this platform)

## 4. QUALITY ASSURANCE FRAMEWORK

### 4.1 AI-to-Doctor Referral Quality

**Triage Accuracy Metrics:**
- Target: 95%+ appropriate referrals
- Monitor: Specialist referrals vs. GP-appropriate cases
- Track: Escalation rate (cases requiring higher-level care)

**Referral Protocol Framework:**

| AI Triage Category | Physician Type | Response Time | Follow-up Required |
|-------------------|----------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Emergency | Immediate escalation to emergency services | <2 minutes | N/A |
| Urgent | On-call physician (any specialty) | <15 minutes | 24-hour follow-up |
| Routine GP | General Practitioner | <30 minutes | As needed |
| Specialist Required | Appropriate specialist | <60 minutes | 48-hour follow-up |
| Mental Health | Psychiatrist/Psychologist | <30 minutes | 1-week follow-up |

### 4.2 Clinical Guidelines and Protocols

**Required Protocols:**
1. **Treatment Protocols:** Evidence-based guidelines for common conditions
2. **Prescription Guidelines:** What can/cannot be prescribed via telemedicine
3. **Escalation Protocols:** When to refer to in-person care
4. **Documentation Standards:** Required information in each consultation

**Restricted Conditions for Telemedicine:**
- First-time controlled substance prescriptions
- Conditions requiring physical examination (acute abdomen, chest pain)
- Emergency conditions requiring immediate intervention
- Certain dermatological conditions requiring biopsy

### 4.3 Patient Outcome Tracking

**Key Metrics:**
- Resolution rate (symptoms resolved without escalation)
- Patient satisfaction score (target: 4.5+/5)
- Follow-up adherence rate
- Re-consultation rate (within 7 days for same condition)
- Escalation to in-person care rate

**Quality Dashboard for Physicians:**
- Consultation volume
- Average consultation duration
- Patient ratings
- Protocol adherence score
- Outcome metrics

### 4.4 Doctor Rating and Feedback System

**Rating Components:**
- Patient satisfaction (1-5 stars)
- Clinical outcome (resolved/escalated)
- Communication quality
- Wait time
- Would recommend (yes/no)

**Quality Thresholds:**
- 4.5+ stars: Quality bonus eligible
- 4.0-4.4 stars: Standard performance
- 3.5-3.9 stars: Performance improvement plan
- Below 3.5: Probation/removal from platform

### 4.5 Handling Misdiagnosis or Errors

**Incident Response Protocol:**
1. Immediate patient safety assessment
2. Incident documentation within 24 hours
3. Peer review within 72 hours
4. Corrective action plan
5. Insurance notification (if applicable)

**Liability Distribution:**
- Physician: Clinical decision-making liability
- Platform: Technology/AI liability, proper referral logic
- Shared: Inadequate protocols, insufficient training

## 5. OPERATIONAL WORKFLOWS

### 5.1 On-Call Scheduling Systems

**Scheduling Best Practices:**
- Use automated scheduling software (e.g., MDsyncNET, OnPage, QGenda)
- Build in recovery time between shifts
- Rotate nights, weekends, and holidays equitably
- Publish schedules 2-4 weeks in advance
- Enable shift swapping with approval

**Recommended Scheduling Model:**

| Shift Type | Hours | Coverage |
|------------|-------|----------|
| Morning Peak | 7:00-11:00 | 40% of daily physicians |
| Midday | 11:00-15:00 | 30% of daily physicians |
| Afternoon Peak | 15:00-19:00 | 40% of daily physicians |
| Evening | 19:00-23:00 | 25% of daily physicians |
| Night | 23:00-7:00 | 15% of daily physicians (on-call) |

**Minimum Coverage Requirements:**
- 2-3 GPs online during peak hours
- 1 specialist per category available within 60 minutes
- 1 on-call physician for overnight urgent cases

### 5.2 Patient Queue Management

**Queue Prioritization:**
1. Emergency (AI-flagged) - Immediate
2. Urgent (symptoms <24 hours) - <15 minutes
3. Routine - <30 minutes
4. Follow-up - <60 minutes

**Queue Management Features:**
- Estimated wait time display
- Option to schedule callback
- Queue position notification
- Alternative physician suggestion if wait exceeds threshold

### 5.3 Consultation Time Standards

**Target Durations:**
| Consultation Type | Target Duration | Maximum Duration |
|-------------------|-----------------|------------------|
| Initial GP consultation | 10-15 minutes | 20 minutes |
| Specialist consultation | 15-20 minutes | 30 minutes |
| Follow-up | 5-10 minutes | 15 minutes |
| Complex case | 20-30 minutes | 45 minutes |

**Quality Metrics:**
- Average consultation time
- Cases exceeding maximum duration (review for efficiency)
- Cases under minimum duration (review for completeness)

### 5.4 Follow-Up Procedures

**Standard Follow-Up Protocol:**
- Acute conditions: 48-72 hours
- Chronic conditions: 1-2 weeks
- Medication adjustments: 1 week
- Specialist referrals: 1 week

**Follow-Up Automation:**
- Automated reminder to patient
- Physician notification of pending follow-up
- Escalation if patient doesn't respond

### 5.5 Prescription Workflows

**Digital Prescription Requirements (Mexico):**
- Article 64 of General Health Law recognizes digital/electronic signatures
- Prescription must include:
  - Patient name and demographics
  - Medication name, dosage, quantity
  - Instructions for use
  - Physician name and cédula number
  - Date
  - Digital signature

**Prescription Delivery Options:**
1. Digital prescription sent to patient (PDF)
2. Direct-to-pharmacy integration
3. QR code for pharmacy scanning

**Controlled Substance Restrictions:**
- Cannot prescribe controlled substances for first-time patients via telemedicine
- Some medications require in-person consultation

### 5.6 Escalation to In-Person Care

**Escalation Triggers:**
- AI flags emergency symptoms
- Physician determines physical exam required
- Diagnostic tests needed (imaging, labs)
- Treatment requires procedure
- Patient preference for in-person care

**Escalation Process:**
1. Physician documents reason for escalation
2. Patient receives referral with:
   - Recommended facility type
   - Urgency level
   - Suggested timeframe
3. Platform tracks referral completion
4. Follow-up consultation scheduled post-visit

## 7. LEGAL CONSIDERATIONS

### 7.1 Doctor Employment vs. Contractor Classification

**Contractor Model (Recommended):**
- Physicians work as independent contractors (prestadores de servicios)
- Platform provides technology and patient referrals
- Physicians control clinical decision-making
- Physicians responsible for own tax obligations
- Lower platform liability exposure

**Key Contract Elements:**
- Scope of services
- Compensation terms
- Quality standards
- Confidentiality (HIPAA-equivalent)
- Liability allocation
- Termination clauses
- Non-compete (limited, if any)

### 7.2 Liability Distribution

**Platform Liability:**
- AI algorithm accuracy
- Technology failures
- Data breaches
- Inadequate physician vetting
- Insufficient protocols

**Physician Liability:**
- Clinical decision-making
- Misdiagnosis
- Prescription errors
- Failure to escalate appropriately
- Documentation deficiencies

**Shared Liability:**
- Inadequate training
- Protocol failures
- Communication breakdowns

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Comprehensive malpractice insurance
- Clear terms of service
- Informed consent from patients
- Regular quality audits
- Incident reporting system

### 7.3 Patient Consent Procedures

**Required Consent Elements (per NOM-004-SSA3-2012):**
1. Nature of telemedicine services
2. Technology used and limitations
3. Privacy and security measures
4. Potential risks
5. Emergency protocols
6. Billing arrangements
7. Right to refuse/withdraw consent
8. How to obtain medical records

**Consent Process:**
- Digital consent form during registration
- Verbal confirmation at first consultation
- Annual re-consent
- Documentation in patient record

**Sample Consent Language (Spanish):**
"Entiendo que la telemedicina implica el uso de tecnología para recibir atención médica a distancia. Entiendo que la calidad de la consulta puede verse afectada por limitaciones tecnológicas y que no reemplaza la atención médica presencial cuando sea necesaria."

### 7.4 Data Privacy Requirements

**Applicable Laws:**
- Federal Law on Protection of Personal Data Held by Private Parties
- General Health Law
- NOM-024-SSA3-2012 (electronic health records)

**Requirements:**
- Privacy notice (aviso de privacidad)
- Patient consent for data processing
- ARCO rights (Access, Rectification, Cancellation, Opposition)
- Data security measures
- Cross-border data transfer restrictions

**Note on INAI:**
- As of November 2024, INAI (data protection authority) is being dissolved
- Functions transferring to new Secretariat of Anti-Corruption and Good Governance
- Monitor regulatory developments

## 10. RECOMMENDATIONS SUMMARY

1. **Start with GPs, add specialists gradually** - Focus on primary care first, then build specialist network based on referral patterns

2. **Competitive but sustainable compensation** - At 40-50% revenue share, physicians can earn MXN 18,000-54,000 monthly, competitive with traditional practice

3. **Invest in malpractice insurance** - Protect both platform and physicians with comprehensive coverage

4. **Build robust quality assurance** - AI-to-doctor handoff is critical; invest in monitoring and improvement

5. **Leverage WhatsApp strategically** - Meet patients where they are, but ensure clinical workflows are robust

6. **Prioritize physician experience** - Happy physicians provide better care; invest in tools, support, and fair compensation

7. **Stay current on regulations** - Mexican telemedicine regulations are evolving; maintain compliance flexibility
