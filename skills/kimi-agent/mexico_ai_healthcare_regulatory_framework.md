# COMPREHENSIVE REGULATORY FRAMEWORK FOR AI-POWERED HEALTHCARE SERVICE IN MEXICO

## Executive Summary

This document provides a complete legal and regulatory analysis for implementing an AI-powered healthcare service in Mexico featuring: (1) AI intake doctor for initial consultations and triage, (2) referral to licensed physicians, (3) pricing at $499 MXN per consultation and $1999 for 6-month subscription, (4) pharmacy partnerships for home delivery, and (5) WhatsApp as the service delivery platform.

**CRITICAL UPDATE (January 2026):** The Mexican government published a major reform to the Ley General de Salud on January 15, 2026, which formally incorporates "Salud Digital" (Digital Health) into Mexican law for the first time. This reform significantly impacts all digital health services.

## 2. NEW DIGITAL HEALTH LAW (JANUARY 2026)

### 2.1 Reform to Ley General de Salud (Published January 15, 2026)

The most significant recent development is the incorporation of **Chapter VI Bis: "Salud Digital"** into the Ley General de Salud.

### 2.2 Key Definitions

**Salud Digital (Digital Health):**
> "The application of information and communication technologies to health services, including telesalud, telemedicine, mobile health, electronic medical records, and portable devices."

**Telesalud (Telesalud):**
> "The provision of health services at a distance, person-centered, including medical guidance, care, education, and research."

**Telemedicina (Telemedicine):**
> "The provision of medical services at a distance to avoid population displacement."

### 2.3 Legal Requirements for Telesalud Services

The reform mandates that ALL telesalud services must comply with:

1. **Trained Personnel**: Services must be provided by specifically designated and trained staff
2. **Secure Systems**: Systems must guarantee integrity and availability of medical information
3. **Informed Consent**: Specific mechanisms for obtaining patient consent for remote care
4. **Documentation**: All care provided through digital platforms must be properly documented
5. **Confidentiality**: Protocols for confidentiality and personal data protection
6. **Interoperability**: Systems must be able to exchange information securely

### 2.4 Strategic Objectives

- Accessibility: Facilitate remote medical services
- Coverage: Expand access, especially in underserved communities
- Efficiency: Optimize human and technological resources
- Continuity and Quality: Digitalize medical information for secure exchange
- Data Analysis: Use Big Data to identify patterns and personalize treatments

## 3. AI MEDICAL CONSULTATION REGULATIONS

### 3.1 Current Legal Status of AI in Healthcare (2025-2026)

**CRITICAL FINDING:** Mexico does NOT currently have specific legislation regulating AI in healthcare. However, several frameworks apply:

### 3.2 Software as a Medical Device (SaMD) Framework

AI systems that provide medical advice may be classified as **SaMD (Software as a Medical Device)** under COFEPRIS regulations.

**Definition of SaMD (Mexican Pharmacopoeia Supplement 5.0):**
> "Software used for one or more medical purposes, which does not need to be integrated with the hardware of the medical device to achieve its intended medical purpose."

### 3.3 Classification of Your AI Intake Doctor

Your AI system conducting initial consultations and triage would likely be classified based on:

| Risk Level | Description | Regulatory Path |
|------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Class I** | Wellness, general health information | No COFEPRIS registration required |
| **Class II** | Diagnostic support, triage | COFEPRIS registration as SaMD required |
| **Class III** | Direct diagnosis or treatment decisions | Full medical device authorization required |

**RECOMMENDATION:** Position your AI as a **triage and information tool** that DOES NOT provide diagnosis or treatment recommendations. The AI should:
- Collect symptoms and medical history
- Provide general health information
- ALWAYS refer to licensed physicians for diagnosis and treatment
- Include clear disclaimers that it is NOT a medical professional

### 3.4 NOM-241-SSA1-2025 (New GMP Standard)

Effective November 30, 2025, NOM-241-SSA1-2025 tightens Good Manufacturing Practice expectations and includes:
- Software validation requirements
- Risk management integration
- Version control for software updates
- Quality management system evidence

### 3.5 AI Legislative Proposals (Pending)

Several AI bills are in discussion:
- **April 2024:** Ley Federal en Materia de Inteligencia Artificial
- **April 2025:** Ley Federal para el Desarrollo Ético, Soberano e Inclusivo de la Inteligencia Artificial

**Key proposed elements:**
- Risk-based classification (unacceptable, high-risk, low-risk)
- Certification mechanisms
- Transparency obligations
- Human oversight requirements

## 5. DIGITAL PRESCRIPTION REGULATIONS

### 5.1 Legal Framework for Electronic Prescriptions

**Current Status:** Electronic prescriptions are permitted under the following framework:

| Regulation | Key Requirements |
|------------|------------------|
| **Ley General de Salud** | General requirements for prescription validity |
| **NOM-024-SSA3-2012** | Functional and interoperability requirements for electronic health records |
| **Ley de Firma Electrónica Avanzada** | Requirements for electronic signatures |
| **COFEPRIS Guidelines** | Specific requirements for controlled substances |

### 5.2 Requirements for Valid Electronic Prescriptions

1. **Electronic/Digital Signature**: Must include the physician's electronic or digital signature
2. **Authentication**: Systems must verify the prescriber's identity
3. **Security**: Encrypted transmission and storage
4. **Traceability**: Complete audit trail from prescription to dispensing
5. **Interoperability**: Must be accepted by authorized pharmacies

### 5.3 Controlled Substances (Fracción I)

For prescribing controlled substances (narcotics, psychotropics):

**COFEPRIS Electronic Prescription System Requirements:**
- Physician registration in COFEPRIS electronic system
- Electronic prescription with barcode
- QR code for validation
- Validation through COFEPRIS portal before dispensing

**Physician Registration Requirements:**
- Be a licensed physician, homeopath, dentist, or veterinarian
- Valid email account
- Scanned official ID
- Certified copy of professional cédula
- Proof of address (less than 3 months old)
- For hospital units: letter from institution director

### 5.4 Pharmacy Integration Requirements

Pharmacies must:
- Validate electronic prescriptions through COFEPRIS system
- Maintain records for 5 years
- Have systems that ensure traceability
- Report dispensing of controlled substances

## 6. DATA PRIVACY AND PROTECTION LAWS

### 6.1 Legal Framework (Mexico's "HIPAA Equivalent")

| Law | Scope | Key Provisions |
|-----|-------|----------------|
| **Ley Federal de Protección de Datos Personales en Posesión de los Particulares (LFPDPPP)** | Private entities | General data protection framework |
| **Ley General de Protección de Datos Personales en Posesión de Sujetos Obligados** | Public entities | Government data protection |
| **NOM-004-SSA3-2012** | Health records | Clinical record confidentiality |
| **NOM-024-SSA3-2012** | Electronic health systems | Technical security requirements |

### 6.2 Health Data as "Sensitive Personal Data"

Under LFPDPPP, health data is classified as **"datos personales sensibles"** (sensitive personal data):

> "Those personal data that affect the most intimate sphere of the data subject, or whose misuse may give rise to discrimination or entail serious risk. Specifically considered sensitive are those that may reveal aspects such as... present and future health status, genetic information..."

### 6.3 Key Compliance Requirements

#### A. Consent Requirements

| Type | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Express Consent** | Required for processing sensitive health data |
| **Written Consent** | Must be documented (can be electronic) |
| **Informed Consent** | Patient must understand how data will be used |
| **Specific Purpose** | Consent must specify the purpose of processing |
| **Revocable** | Patient can revoke consent at any time |

**Article 10, Section VI LFPDPPP Exception:**
> Consent is NOT required when data processing is "indispensable for medical care, prevention, diagnosis, provision of health assistance, medical treatments or health services management, while the data subject is not in a position to give consent... provided that such processing is carried out by a person subject to professional secrecy."

#### B. Privacy Notice (Aviso de Privacidad) Requirements

Your privacy notice MUST include:
- Identity and address of the data controller
- Purposes of data processing (primary and secondary)
- Types of data collected
- Options for limiting use or disclosure
- Mechanisms to exercise ARCO rights
- Data transfer disclosures
- Procedure to revoke consent
- Date of last update

#### C. ARCO Rights (Patient Rights)

Patients have the right to:
- **A**ccess their personal data
- **R**ectify incorrect or incomplete data
- **C**ancel their data when no longer necessary
- **O**ppose processing for specific purposes

#### D. Security Requirements

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Administrative Safeguards** | Security policies, access controls, training |
| **Physical Safeguards** | Secure facilities, access restrictions |
| **Technical Safeguards** | Encryption, firewalls, intrusion detection |
| **Pseudonymization** | Recommended for sensitive data |

#### E. Data Transfer and Cross-Border Processing

- Cross-border transfers require express consent OR contractual clauses
- Must document security measures in data processing agreements
- Cloud storage location matters - patient data should remain in Mexico when possible

### 6.4 NOM-024-SSA3-2012 Technical Requirements

For electronic health record systems:

1. **Interoperability**: Exchange information with other systems
2. **Security**: Protection against unauthorized access
3. **Data Integrity**: Guarantee information is not modified without authorization
4. **Access Traceability**: Log who accesses, views, or modifies data
5. **Confidentiality**: Technical mechanisms to protect sensitive information
6. **Authentication**: Verify user identity before granting access
7. **Encrypted Communication**: Secure data transmission

## 7. ADVERTISING RESTRICTIONS

### 7.1 COFEPRIS Advertising Authorization Framework

Under the Reglamento de la Ley General de Salud en Materia de Publicidad:

| Service/Product | Authorization Type |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Health Services | **Permiso de Publicidad** (Advertising Permit) |
| Medical Devices | Permiso or Aviso depending on target audience |
| Medications | Permiso de Publicidad |
| Telemedicine Platforms | Permiso de Publicidad |

### 7.2 Permiso de Publicidad Requirements

**Required for:**
- Health services (when promoted to the general public)
- Medical devices
- Telemedicine platforms

**Application Requirements:**
- Completed COFEPRIS-02-001 form
- Advertising project/materials
- Documentation supporting claims
- Valid sanitary license
- Fee payment

### 7.3 Prohibited Advertising Content

Under COFEPRIS guidelines, advertising CANNOT:
- Make claims about curing diseases without scientific evidence
- Promise specific results or guarantees
- Use testimonials that create false expectations
- Target vulnerable populations with misleading information
- Minimize risks or side effects
- Compare products/services in a disparaging way

### 7.4 Digital Advertising Specifics

For WhatsApp and digital platforms:
- All digital advertising requires COFEPRIS authorization
- Must include registration number
- Cannot use pop-ups or forced viewing techniques
- Must be clearly identifiable as advertising
- Must include risk information where applicable

### 7.5 Penalties for Non-Compliance

- Administrative fines
- Order to cease advertising
- Suspension of sanitary registrations
- Criminal liability in severe cases

## 8. CONSUMER PROTECTION FOR SUBSCRIPTION SERVICES

### 8.1 Ley Federal de Protección al Consumidor (LFPC)

Your $499 MXN per consultation and $1999 for 6-month subscription pricing must comply with:

### 8.2 Contract Requirements

**For your subscription service, you MUST provide:**

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **Clear Pricing** | Total cost including taxes, fees |
| **Service Description** | Exactly what is included in subscription |
| **Duration** | Clear subscription period (6 months) |
| **Renewal Terms** | How renewal works and how to cancel |
| **Cancellation Policy** | Process and any penalties |
| **Refund Policy** | Conditions for refunds |

### 8.3 Contratos de Adhesión (Adhesion Contracts)

Your terms of service constitute a "contrato de adhesión" under Article 85 of LFPC:

> "The document prepared unilaterally by the provider to establish in uniform formats the terms and conditions applicable to the acquisition of a product or the provision of a service."

**Requirements:**
- Written in Spanish
- Legible font size and type
- Cannot contain abusive or inequitable clauses
- Cannot allow unilateral modification by provider
- Must be registered with PROFECO if required by NOM

### 8.4 Consumer Rights Under LFPC

Your customers have the right to:

1. **Information**: Clear, truthful, sufficient information about services
2. **Non-Discrimination**: Equal treatment
3. **Safety**: Services that don't endanger health
4. **Education**: Information to make informed decisions
5. **Redress**: Mechanisms to resolve disputes
6. **Cancellation**: Ability to cancel subscription (with conditions)

### 8.5 Prohibited Clauses (Article 90)

Your contracts CANNOT include:
- Clauses allowing unilateral modification by provider
- Clauses exempting provider from liability
- Clauses waiving consumer rights
- Automatic renewal without clear notice
- Excessive cancellation penalties

### 8.6 Pricing Transparency Requirements

For your pricing model ($499 consultation / $1999 subscription):
- Must display prices in Mexican Pesos (MXN)
- Must include VAT (IVA) or clearly state if excluded
- Cannot have hidden fees
- Must explain what each service tier includes
- Subscription terms must be clear and prominent

## 9. PHARMACY PARTNERSHIP REGULATIONS

### 9.1 Pharmacy Licensing Requirements

Any pharmacy partner MUST have:

| License/Permit | Issuing Authority | Purpose |
|----------------|-------------------|---------|
| **Aviso de Funcionamiento** | COFEPRIS | Authorization to operate |
| **Licencia Sanitaria** | COFEPRIS | Required for controlled substances |
| **Responsable Sanitario** | COFEPRIS | Licensed pharmacist on staff |
| **RFC** | SAT | Tax registration |

### 9.2 Requirements for Pharmacy Operation

**Physical Requirements:**
- Minimum distance from other pharmacies (approximately 200 meters)
- Adequate space for storage and dispensing
- Proper infrastructure for medication storage
- Visible and accessible location

**Personnel Requirements:**
- Licensed pharmacist as "Responsable Sanitario"
- Minimum high school education for staff
- Annual SICAD training (Sistema Integral de Capacitación)
- Clean uniforms and identification badges
- Non-professional staff CANNOT provide medical advice

### 9.3 Home Delivery Regulations

**Current Status:** Home delivery of medications is permitted but regulated:

**Requirements:**
1. **Good Distribution Practices**: Must comply with NOM-059-SSA1-2015 Chapter 16
2. **Temperature Control**: Products must be maintained at labeled conditions
3. **Product Integrity**: Must guarantee identification and integrity
4. **Distribution Records**: Maintain records of each lot for potential recalls
5. **Delivery Personnel**: Must be trained in handling medications

**NOM-059-SSA1-2015 Key Requirements:**
- Storage conditions (temperature, humidity)
- FIFO/FEFO inventory management
- Proper handling during transport
- Documentation of delivery chain
- Returns and complaints handling

### 9.4 Controlled Substance Delivery

**CRITICAL:** Controlled substances (Fracción I) have additional restrictions:
- Cannot be delivered without proper prescription validation
- Must validate through COFEPRIS electronic system
- May require patient identification at delivery
- Special handling and chain of custody requirements

### 9.5 Partnership Agreement Requirements

Your pharmacy partnership agreements should include:
- Scope of services
- Quality standards
- Delivery timeframes
- Liability allocation
- Data sharing protocols (patient data protection)
- Insurance requirements
- Compliance with COFEPRIS regulations
- Audit rights

## 10. MEDICAL MALPRACTICE AND LIABILITY

### 10.1 Legal Framework for Medical Liability

**CONAMED (Comisión Nacional de Arbitraje Médico)**

The January 2026 reform strengthened CONAMED as:
> "An administrative body with full technical and operational autonomy to intervene in conciliation, mediation, and arbitration in the resolution of disputes between users and health service providers."

### 10.2 Types of Medical Liability

Physicians can be liable for:

| Type | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Negligencia** | Failure to follow basic principles of the profession |
| **Impericia** | Lack of basic skills or knowledge required |
| **Imprudencia** | Taking unnecessary risks without proper precautions |

### 10.3 Malpractice Insurance

**Current Status in Mexico:**
- Malpractice insurance is **NOT legally mandatory** for all physicians in Mexico
- However, it is **STRONGLY RECOMMENDED** and often required by:
  - Hospitals and clinics
  - Medical groups
  - Some professional associations

**For Your Platform:**
- Require physicians to maintain malpractice insurance
- Consider platform-level liability insurance
- Document insurance coverage for all physicians

### 10.4 Liability Allocation

**Critical Issue:** AI systems CANNOT be held medically liable in Mexico.

**Recommended Liability Structure:**
1. **AI System**: Information/triage tool only
2. **Physician**: Full medical liability for diagnosis and treatment
3. **Platform**: Administrative and technical liability
4. **Pharmacy**: Liability for dispensing errors

### 10.5 Informed Consent Requirements

Your platform MUST obtain informed consent that includes:
- Nature of telemedicine services
- Limitations of remote consultation
- Technology used (WhatsApp)
- Privacy and data handling
- Physician credentials
- Costs and payment terms
- Right to refuse or discontinue
- Complaint mechanisms (CONAMED)

### 10.6 CONAMED Complaint Process

Patients can file complaints with CONAMED:
- In person at Av. Marina Nacional #60, Piso 14, Col. Tacuba, CDMX
- By phone: (55) 5420 7000
- By email: primera_atencion@conamed.gob.mx
- Online through CONAMED web form

**Your platform should:**
- Provide CONAMED contact information to patients
- Have internal complaint handling procedures
- Document all patient interactions
- Maintain records for at least 5 years

## 11. WHATSAPP AS HEALTHCARE PLATFORM

### 11.1 Legal Considerations

**Current Status:** There is NO specific prohibition on using WhatsApp for healthcare in Mexico. However:

### 11.2 Limitations and Risks

| Issue | Risk | Mitigation |
|-------|------|------------|
| **No Physical Examination** | Cannot verify patient identity or condition | Require video consultation for diagnosis |
| **Message Encryption** | WhatsApp encryption is good for privacy but limits record-keeping | Implement parallel documentation system |
| **Data Storage** | WhatsApp stores data on Meta servers (potentially outside Mexico) | Minimize PHI shared on WhatsApp |
| **Message Disappearance** | Messages can be deleted | Automated backup to compliant system |
| **Platform Dependency** | WhatsApp can suspend accounts | Have alternative communication channels |

### 11.3 NOM-004 Compliance on WhatsApp

According to medical legal analysis:
> "There is NO obligation for physicians to respond to remote text messages to provide medical indications... NOM-004-SSA3-2012 never contemplates that medical care can be provided through instant messaging."

**Recommendation:** Use WhatsApp for:
- Scheduling and reminders
- General information
- Initial triage (AI)
- Follow-up communications (non-clinical)

NOT for:
- Formal diagnosis
- Treatment prescriptions (without video consultation)
- Emergency care
- Controlled substance prescriptions

### 11.4 Technical Requirements for WhatsApp Integration

1. **Business API**: Use WhatsApp Business API, not personal accounts
2. **Data Backup**: Automatically backup all conversations to compliant storage
3. **Access Controls**: Limit who can access patient conversations
4. **Audit Trail**: Log all interactions
5. **Consent**: Obtain explicit consent for WhatsApp communication

## 14. LEGAL RISKS AND MITIGATION

### 14.1 High-Risk Areas

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| **AI Misdiagnosis** | Medium | Very High | Clear disclaimers; physician oversight; limit AI to triage only |
| **Data Breach** | Medium | Very High | Encryption; access controls; incident response plan; cyber insurance |
| **Physician Malpractice** | Medium | High | Insurance requirements; credential verification; clear protocols |
| **COFEPRIS Sanctions** | Low | High | Proper registration; compliance monitoring; legal counsel |
| **CONAMED Complaints** | Medium | Medium | Clear consent; documentation; dispute resolution procedures |
| **Consumer Protection Violations** | Low | Medium | PROFECO registration; clear contracts; fair practices |

### 14.2 Specific Risk: AI Providing Medical Advice

**Risk:** If your AI is perceived as providing medical diagnosis or treatment recommendations without physician oversight, you could face:
- COFEPRIS sanctions for operating without authorization
- Medical liability claims
- Criminal liability for practicing medicine without license

**Mitigation:**
1. Position AI as "health information and triage tool" only
2. Always route to licensed physician for diagnosis
3. Include prominent disclaimers
4. Document that AI does not replace medical consultation
5. Consider SaMD registration if AI provides any diagnostic support

### 14.3 Specific Risk: WhatsApp Data Privacy

**Risk:** WhatsApp stores data on Meta servers, potentially outside Mexico, which could violate data localization requirements.

**Mitigation:**
1. Minimize PHI shared through WhatsApp
2. Use WhatsApp for scheduling and general info only
3. Implement parallel secure system for clinical data
4. Obtain explicit consent for WhatsApp use
5. Consider Mexican-hosted messaging alternatives

### 14.4 Specific Risk: Subscription Service Cancellation

**Risk:** Mexican consumer protection law requires clear cancellation mechanisms.

**Mitigation:**
1. Clear cancellation policy in terms
2. Easy cancellation process
3. Prorated refund policy
4. No excessive penalties
5. Document all cancellation requests

### 14.5 Recommended Legal Structure

**Corporate Structure:**
- Mexican corporation (Sociedad Anónima or S. de R.L.)
- Separate legal entity for liability protection
- Proper capitalization
- Board of directors with healthcare expertise

**Insurance Coverage:**
- General liability insurance
- Cyber liability insurance
- Professional liability (for platform)
- Require physicians to maintain malpractice insurance

**Legal Counsel:**
- Retain Mexican healthcare law firm
- Regular compliance audits
- Updates on regulatory changes
- Representation before COFEPRIS
